# Decision Modeling for Accountable AI

As software systems scale, core business logic often becomes difficult to maintain. It frequently ends up buried within application code—requiring full deployments for simple rule changes—or handed over to unpredictable AI models. When a system automates decisions around risk, eligibility, or pricing, organizations must be able to explain exactly why a choice was made. Achieving accountability requires moving away from hardcoded logic and black-box models toward a transparent approach to decision management.

In this session, we will explore how to build reliable, auditable automation. Using an open-source reference implementation from the insurance industry, we will walk through the design patterns needed to externalize complex business rules into explicit, human-readable decision models. You will see how this separation of concerns allows business analysts to manage live logic directly, freeing engineers to focus on core infrastructure. Finally, we will examine the operational benefits of local, deterministic automation, showing how it delivers fast, compliant results without the costs or unpredictability of large language models.

Key Takeaways:
* Architectural Decoupling: How to isolate business rules from application code to improve agility and reduce technical debt.
* Auditable Logic: Strategies for creating versioned, human-readable decision models that satisfy compliance requirements.
* Separation of Concerns: Empowering business experts to safely manage logic while developers focus on infrastructure.
* Practical Alternatives to LLMs: When and why deterministic automation outperforms probabilistic AI in speed, reliability, and cost.
